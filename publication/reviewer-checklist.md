# Reviewer Checklist

Use this file to sanity-check the paper against the review form you shared.

## Review form items

1. I have read the reviewer guidelines.
   - `Yes / No`

2. I am an expert on the topic of the paper.
   - `Strongly agree / Agree / Disagree / Strongly disagree`

3. The title and abstract reflect the content of the paper.
   - `Strongly agree / Agree / Disagree / Strongly disagree`

4. The paper discusses, cites and compares with all relevant related work.
   - `Strongly agree / Agree / Disagree / Strongly disagree`

5. Please justify the previous choice.
   - Write `n/a` unless you selected `Disagree` or `Strongly disagree`.

6. Readability and paper organization.
   - `Strongly agree / Agree / Disagree / Strongly disagree`

7. The paper adheres to ISMIR submission guidelines.
   - `Yes / No`

8. Scholarly/scientific quality.
   - `Agree / Disagree`

9. Novelty of the paper.
   - `Agree / Disagree`

10. The paper provides all the necessary details or material to reproduce the results described in the paper.
    - `Agree / Disagree`

11. Reusable insights.
    - `Agree / Disagree` plus a brief explanation.

12. One-line take-home message from the paper.

13. Overall evaluation.
    - `Strong accept / Weak accept / Weak reject / Reject`

14. Comments to the authors.

## Suggested structure for comments to the authors

1. Paper summary
2. Strengths
3. Weaknesses
4. Presentation
5. Methodology
6. Evaluation
7. Impact and significance
8. Technical quality / reproducibility

## How the current draft should answer this checklist

- Title and abstract:
  - The draft should clearly state that this is a symbolic benchmark for music theory and computational musicology.
- Related work:
  - Expand symbolic music modeling, music benchmark design, and computational musicology references before submission.
- Readability:
  - Keep the benchmark contribution distinct from the musicological framing.
- Scientific quality:
  - Avoid overstating model-understanding claims; present the benchmark as a probe, not a proof of understanding.
- Novelty:
  - Emphasize task operationalization, unified evaluation, and the correctness-vs-explanation distinction.
- Reproducibility:
  - Point reviewers to the scripts, canonical prediction schema, local web UI, and Pages dashboard.
